Validation Chain (Ch1→Ch2→Ch3)
  1. Define Credibility Veneer as a nudge carrier: Ch1 establishes institutional-trust shortcuts (brand/recommendation reliance). In Ch2, official logos, doctor-endorsed labels, and medical tone are treated as deliberate nudge vehicles that hijack System-1 trust heuristics rather than neutral decoration (Thaler & Sunstein logic).
  2. Use Yu-Chen 2024 as bridge evidence: Yu & Chen shows natural chatbot dialogue lowers technology anxiety and that social influence is a dominant acceptance driver. Chain: Ch1 trust shortcut exists → natural dialogue reduces defensive friction → Ch2 hypernudge can reshape choice environment faster than users can verify risk.
  3. Static shortcut to dynamic manipulation: Ch1 trust is mostly static (e.g., "see logo, assume safe"). Ch2 upgrades it into dynamic, personalized nudging via AI-powered interaction loops (Duane 2025), explaining why legacy trust cues fail under agentic systems.

Ch2 conclusion → Ch3 start: veneer-driven trust can rise while real risk-check evidence remains absent. Therefore Ch3 introduces the HITL proxy-auditor requirement to force explicit, inspectable risk-check evidence.
