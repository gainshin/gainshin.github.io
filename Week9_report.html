Why Framework Version 5 Was Created

Version 5 was created after advisor feedback exposed a strategic mismatch in the prior framing: the project still spread effort across multiple intervention surfaces and chapter-level ambitions, while the feasible contribution is one implementable HITL interaction pattern.

  • Problem correction: Previous framing over-indexed on a chapter-structured proof strategy; V5 re-centers on one buildable research target.
  • Mechanism correction: Endorsement effects were reframed as a general credibility-shortcut behavior, not an age-specific phenomenon.
  • Implementation correction: Proxy auditing was moved from abstract architecture language to an explicit interface pattern (capture once, replay responsibly, escalate when needed).

RQ supplement: expanded caregiver definition in V5

A critical V5 adjustment (aligned with advisor consensus) is that the proxy-auditor role is no longer treated as family-only delegation. Caregiver remains central, but is explicitly expanded to a broader proxy-auditor set:

  • Family caregivers
  • Family physicians / clinicians
  • Institutional reviewers
  • Trusted peer / community channels

This change matters for RQ scope: trust calibration and risk-check evidence must support heterogeneous proxy roles, not only one household caregiver pathway.

Validation Chain (Ch1→Ch2→Ch3)

  • Define Credibility Veneer as a nudge carrier: Ch1 establishes institutional-trust shortcuts (brand/recommendation reliance). In Ch2, official logos, doctor-endorsed labels, and medical tone are treated as deliberate nudge vehicles that hijack System-1 trust heuristics rather than neutral decoration (Thaler & Sunstein logic).
  • Use Yu-Chen 2024 as bridge evidence: Yu & Chen shows natural chatbot dialogue lowers technology anxiety and that social influence is a dominant acceptance driver. Chain: Ch1 trust shortcut exists → natural dialogue reduces defensive friction → Ch2 hypernudge can reshape choice environment faster than users can verify risk.
  • Static shortcut to dynamic manipulation: Ch1 trust is mostly static (e.g., "see logo, assume safe"). Ch2 upgrades it into dynamic, personalized nudging via AI-powered interaction loops (Duane 2025), explaining why legacy trust cues fail under agentic systems.

Ch2 conclusion → Ch3 start: veneer-driven trust can rise while real risk-check evidence remains absent. Therefore Ch3 introduces the HITL proxy-auditor requirement to force explicit, inspectable risk-check evidence.

Why Design Target A Was Removed in V5

Design Target A (veneer-resistant trust calibration) was removed from V5 as a standalone target to prevent a split-build problem. Keeping both A and B as independent implementation tracks in the same timeline increased design surface area and weakened empirical tractability.

  • Method reason: One prototype target is easier to validate rigorously than two partially implemented targets.
  • Scope reason: A did not disappear conceptually; it was absorbed as constraints/guardrails inside Ch2 and Ch4 boundary logic (provenance, audit trail, expiry/revalidation).
  • Evaluation reason: The Week 9 direction prioritizes measurable outcomes for Target B (capture-and-replay effectiveness, escalation behavior, and support-load reduction).
