Does ChatGPT Treat Every Senior the Same?

Introduction
Introduction & Background
"ChatGPT fields millions of health queries daily — yet it treats a 70-year-old retired nurse identically to a 70-year-old with limited literacy."

Older adults are not monolithic. Literacy, digital fluency, and medical knowledge create vastly different risk profiles (Dai et al., 2021).

AI systems use "hypernudges" — personalised, real-time persuasive design (Duane et al., 2025) — that can dangerously mislead vulnerable users while genuinely helping savvy ones.

Current research defaults to a one-size-fits-all deficit narrative. This study proposes to examine the spectrum.

Research Question
How do hypernudge and halo-effect patterns in Reddit ChatGPT health discussions differ according to users’ health literacy cues?
Framework
Framework & Literature

The literature reveals 4 converging layers:

  • Heterogeneity — older adults vary widely in literacy & digital fluency (Dai et al., 2021; Ellis et al., 2025)
  • Hypernudges — AI delivers personalised, real-time persuasion (Duane et al., 2025)
  • Seamful Design — should we expose the AI's limitations? (Gull et al., 2025)
  • Contestability — can the user challenge or override the AI? (Vaithilingam et al., 2025)

Literature Review & Framework Synthesis

Data: First-person health narratives from r/ChatGPT, r/Aging, r/AgingParents — naturally occurring literacy cues, not surveys.

Planned Analysis: Two-cycle manual content analysis. Cycle 1: line-by-line open codes. Cycle 2: axial categories aligned to the 4-layer framework.

Unit: Each thread (OP + replies) = one case, enabling cross-case comparison across literacy levels.

Rigour: Supervisor codebook review; saturation log; reflexive journal to guard against stereotypical framing.

Significance
Status & Next Steps
Filling a Gap
The literature suggests high-literacy elders may benefit from ChatGPT health triage, while low-literacy users risk blind trust. This nuance is absent from existing research — our proposed study aims to fill it.
Design Implications
The 4-layer framework can point to concrete fixes — like revealing AI limitations to vulnerable users — informing how we build health AI going forward.

Anticipated Questions

"Reinforcing stereotypes?" No — we reject deficit framing. Focus is literacy as spectrum, not age as deficit.
"Older adults too broad?" Exactly the point. Within-group heterogeneity exceeds between-group differences (Dai et al., 2021).
"n=25 too small?" QCA prioritises depth. Saturation tracking validates 20–30 threads for exploratory research.
"Reddit not representative?" Acknowledged — but naturally occurring narratives = ecological validity. Future work: triangulate with interviews.
