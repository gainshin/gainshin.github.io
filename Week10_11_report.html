Week 10–11 Progress Report

Method draft aligned with Comparative Structured Observation (CSO; Mackay & McGrenere, 2025), HelpCall as a practice precedent (Tanprasert et al., 2024), and the V5 formative chapters (1–3) as the design-concept grounding.

Method

Methodological framework: Comparative Structured Observation

This study adopts Comparative Structured Observation (CSO; Mackay & McGrenere, 2025) as the overarching method. CSO is

an interventionist, qualitative method for assessing and advancing a design concept where researchers observe participants as they compare and reflect deeply upon their experiences with selected design variants, exposure to which is structured according to experimental design principles

(Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, p. 4). Comparison is central: participants experience two or more design variants under structured conditions, and researchers capture their comparative reflections through observation and interviews to generate implications for design. CSO borrows the structure of experimental design (e.g., counterbalancing) to control presentation order but does not borrow the quantitative measurement logic or causal inference goals of experiments — qualitative analysis is always primary (Criterion 10, Table 2, Mackay & McGrenere, 2025).

HelpCall (Tanprasert et al., 2024) is a published practice instance of CSO. McGrenere, co-author of HelpCall, is also the co-originator of the CSO method. HelpCall used a within-subjects design with N = 14 to compare two augmented steps display candidates (Tooltip vs. List), with qualitative analysis as primary and quantitative metrics as secondary — an instance of CSO in practice. The present study extends this design-comparison logic within the same methodological framework.

Theoretical lens

This study draws on Lee and See’s (2004) trust calibration framework as a theoretical lens to organize the literature review, but the research question itself does not presuppose a “correct” trust level. The analysis will not judge whether participants’ trust is “correctly calibrated” but will describe the paths and textures of their trust reasoning.

CSO criterion numbering in Figure 2 follows Mackay and McGrenere’s Comparative Structured Observation checklist (Table 2, Criteria 1–12) in ACM TOCHI 32(2) [Mackay & McGrenere, 2025].

Study design

See Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, Table 2, p. 14:8–14:9, Criteria 1–12.

This study employs a CSO one-factor within-participants design comparing two replay formats (Format A vs. Format B), corresponding to CSO Criterion 2, variant type (1): “Two or more variants of a novel design concept” (Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, p. 5). The design concept is grounded in the formative research reported in Chapters 1–3 (Criterion 1). Format presentation order will be counterbalanced across participants to mitigate primacy and order effects (Criterion 4). Scenario vignettes supply structured review tasks that keep conditions comparable across formats (Criterion 3). Semi-structured interviews explicitly require participants to compare their experiences with the two formats (Criterion 5). Think-aloud protocols and screen recordings capture participants’ interactions with each format as an independent behavioral data source (Criterion 6). Quantitative data, if collected, will serve only to contextualize qualitative findings (Criterion 7). Analysis will use reflexive thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006), a recognized qualitative method (Criteria 8–10). Findings will explicitly discuss implications for design and how the Proxy Auditor concept should evolve (Criterion 12).

Participants

Participants will review replay records of AI health recommendations in simulated scenarios, using think-aloud to show how they notice, refer to, and draw on proxy audit evidence when reasoning about trust. Recruitment will rely on purposive and snowball sampling to capture diversity in reasoning approaches. HelpCall similarly used “convenience and snowball sampling, as well as advertisements” (Tanprasert et al., 2024, p. 7).

The target sample is 10–14 participants. CSO’s four published case studies (Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, Table 3) range from 10 to 16 participants; HelpCall completed a qualitative comparison of two design candidates with N = 14, identifying three themes and cross-format design trade-offs. CSO does not target statistical inference; it seeks “rich, contextualized insights about design variants” (Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, p. 6), so sample size will be guided by the richness of qualitative insight rather than statistical power. Following HelpCall’s approach, a rolling analysis strategy will be adopted: initial coding will begin after the first 6–8 participants to identify preliminary thematic directions, with subsequent interviews probing deeper into emerging themes. Thematic saturation, rather than a fixed number, will serve as the stopping criterion. Baghestani et al.’s (2024) collaborative learning dynamics study with older adults provides an additional precedent at a similar scale.

Following HelpCall’s collection of demographics and technology experience (Table 2, Tanprasert et al., 2024), this study will record participants’ age, educational background, self-rated digital literacy, and prior exposure to AI health tools to contextualize individual differences in qualitative findings.

Materials

Two replay formats (Format A vs. Format B)

Two structurally different presentations of the same AI health recommendation interaction. Both present identical underlying information but differ in presentation structure — following HelpCall’s Tooltip vs. List logic. Specific format designs will be finalized during prototyping (possible dimensions include: verbatim transcript vs. summary; chronological vs. thematic organization; with/without seamful markers). CSO requires that design variants avoid “straw-man comparisons” (Table 2, “good” criteria); both formats must remain plausible design options.

Scenario vignettes

Two to three simulated AI health agent interaction scenarios that embed varying degrees of proxy audit evidence (e.g., AI inferential premises, uncertainty markers, endorsement signals). CSO requires tasks “chosen to maximize ecological validity and generate qualitative data relevant to the design concept” (Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, p. 5). Scenarios will be designed from the formative research in Chapters 1–3, and participants will be screened to ensure the scenarios are novel to them.

Medium-fidelity prototypes

CSO states that “Design variants are usually expressed as medium-fidelity prototypes” and “must be sufficiently mature to capture how users will experience different aspects of the design concept through performing relevant tasks” (Mackay & McGrenere, 2025, p. 5). This study will use medium-fidelity interactive prototypes at the concept-validation stage, with that limitation stated explicitly.

Semi-structured interview guide

Probes how participants notice, refer to, and draw on evidence across formats, and how trust and distrust manifest in their reasoning.

Think-aloud protocol

Collected concurrently during replay review.

Procedure (60–75 minutes total)

  1. Introduction and informed consent (10 min).
  2. Participants work through two replay formats in sequence (order counterbalanced), each paired with a scenario vignette, with concurrent think-aloud (30–40 min).
  3. Comparative reflection interview: semi-structured interview explicitly asking participants to compare their experiences with the two formats, focusing on differences in noticing, referring to, and drawing on evidence (15–20 min).
  4. Member-check summary (5 min).

Positionality statement

Following HelpCall’s positionality statement (Tanprasert et al., 2024, Section 4.4), the researcher’s HCI and privacy research background, stance toward AI health systems, and how these may influence interview design and data interpretation will be explicitly reported. Throughout data collection and analysis, the researcher will note positionality and revisit the validity of interpretations.

Ethical considerations

All participants will provide written informed consent that specifies purpose, data use, and the right to withdraw. Scenario vignettes concern medical decisions, which may cause discomfort for some participants; the introduction will include information on support resources. The study will be submitted to the McGill REB, with an estimated 8-week review timeline. TCPS2 CORE training is complete (Certificate #0001446233).
