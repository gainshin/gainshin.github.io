Zotero Report — Round 5

Round 5 consolidates all grouped papers from prior rounds into a Tier Evaluation, applying the three-dimensional framework (Usability / Transparency / Persuasive Design) and core RQ alignment (dark patterns, health chatbots, older adults, AI transparency) to classify each key paper as Tier 1 (core pillar), Tier 2 (supplementary), or Unaccepted.

Tier Classification Criteria (per Round 3/4 standards)
  • Tier 1 — Core theoretical/empirical pillar: full three-dimensional coverage (Usability + Transparency + Persuasive Design), directly supports core RQ, HCI depth analysis, non-general review (e.g., Gray 2018).
  • Tier 2 — Supplementary: strong on 2 of 3 dimensions, partial RQ alignment, fills specific gaps but not central to the concept chain.
  • Unaccepted — Incomplete three-dimensional coverage, non-interface focus, or general review only lacking three-dimensional depth.

Core Solution Chain

These papers form the core solution chain directly supporting the development of an Audit Tool and design heuristics for older adults' healthcare AI:

Seamful Design (Expose Issues) → Positive Friction (Trigger Reflection) → Contestability (Enable Correction)
